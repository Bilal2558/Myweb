Types of Rebars Used to Strengthen Steel Structures

Steel in the Philippines is one of the strongest types of construction materials available, providing your buildings and projects with a structurally sound base to build upon. While the durability and strength of steel are able to withstand high temperatures, wear and tear, and heavy amounts of weight regularly, there are certain scenarios where reinforcing steel structures is necessary.

Reinforcing steel is a process that can usually be found in some of the bigger and more ambitious construction projects, where certain sensitive and important areas require extra strength and durability in order to make the structure stand. One of the most common and efficient ways to increase the strength of steel structures is through the use of rebar, which is a form of steel that can help it become stronger, especially with steel that is mixed with concrete.

 

Types of Rebar

A rebar may come in different forms, each providing the same purpose of reinforcing structures. However, knowing which type to use will allow you to find the best type that would help strengthen your structure. Each type of rebar has its own properties that make them a great fit for specific structures as well. Cost is also a big factor, as your project might need the best balance to accommodate all of the necessary materials. Here are the types of rebar commonly used in construction.

 

Deformed Steel Bar

The most common form of steel rebar, deformed steel bars are primarily used to reinforce steel sections that have been encased in concrete. Along with having high tensile strength, these bars are very flexible and easy to weld and maintain. When it comes to strengthening different kinds of structures, deformed steel bars are the best option to take. Most projects that use steel in the Philippines have these types of rebars at the ready, as they are abundant and easy to install to almost any kind of structure.

 

Mild Steel Bar

Unlike deformed steel bars, mild steel bars are plain and round in shape. Used for columns and road projects, these mild steel bars are smaller in size, and are very easy to cut and bend, making it easy to install. For lighter and smaller types of construction projects, mild steel bars are a great fit.

 

European Rebar

A low-cost rebar option, these types of bars are made out of manganese, making them very flexible to use. However, their flexibility also comes at a disadvantage as these bars are not durable enough to withstand various natural phenomena like earthquakes and hurricanes. If you are looking for an affordable rebar option for smaller projects, European rebars are the best option.

 

Carbon Steel Rebar

One of the most durable rebar options around, carbon steel rebars are light and very flexible, making them excellent choices for different kinds of construction projects. Despite their durable properties, the only drawback of using carbon steel rebars is that they are susceptible to corrosion, which means that constant maintenance is highly required.

 

Epoxy Coated Rebar

When it comes to protecting your structures against corrosion, using an epoxy coated rebar is the best option. This type of rebar is your standard steel bar that is reinforced with an epoxy coating that makes it highly resistant to different types of corrosion, while still having the same tensile strength as your standard type of rebar. The only drawback of this type of rebar is that the coating can fade, which can make the steel more susceptible to corrosion over time. This requires constant re-applying of protective coating in order to remain safe from corrosion.

 

Galvanized Rebar

One of the most durable and corrosion-resistant types of rebar, galvanized rebar is the best options if you are looking to strengthen your steel structure immensely. This type of rebar is also a more expensive option to take but provides the best protection from corrosion and natural events. The strong layer of galvanized rebar is difficult to damage, allowing you to use it for bigger and heavier projects.

 

Stainless Steel Rebar

Arguably the most expensive rebar option, stainless steel rebars are some of the best options in the market. Being one of the best types of metal when it comes to resisting corrosion, stainless steel rebars are highly resistant to various elements, making them great to use in outdoor and exposed areas. They are also very flexible and resistant to high temperatures as well, making them great for industrial areas. When it comes to drawbacks, the cost of using this type of rebar can make it a very difficult option to obtain.

 

Key Takeaway

Rebars are the best types of steel parts that help strengthen and enforce steel structures. Knowing each type of steel rebar will allow you to identify which type is the best for your steel structures, as you would always want the best balance between strength and flexibility.
